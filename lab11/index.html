Challenge

This lab focuses on us dipping our toes into more complex block styling, including dropdowns, side content, and things more complex than a page that is just full of paragraphs and links. The previously explored "image float right" is being expanded upon to make a website of greater complexity overall and that will, with some effort, allow us to create something more modern and user friendly than just a lot of text in a row.

Problems

Right off the bat, this entire topic seems intimidating, what with the addition of tags like Main and specific spacing and areas for different content. Also just how a lot of these newer concepts work with manupulating block elements seem really complicated and confusing, although they probably are a whole lot easier to use than they are to explain if past lessons were anything to consider. Mostly I feel like I'm just confused as to why and how specific things like float work, and where tags like fixed, absolute, and reletive apply. Mostly that will come in practice I believe. In spite of everything and my confusion, I still got things to work in the end, even if I don't exactly understand everything of what I did.

Results

I feel like there is almost definitely a cleaner way to make and impliment what I have here, and I am not at all positive if I managed to correctly format the sidebar of information, looking at it after all this. That aside, I'm happy it wasnt entirely intimidating in practice and overall.
